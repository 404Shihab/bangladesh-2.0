“The July Movement: A New Dawn”

The July Revolution,also known as the July Mass Uprising, Gen Z Revolution or Student-People's Uprising, was a mass uprising in Bangladesh in 2024. It began as a quota reform movement to reform the quota rule for government jobs, after the Supreme Court of Bangladesh invalidated the government's 2018 circular regarding job quotas in the public sector. It began in early June 2024 and was led by the Students Against Discrimination. Coinciding with the first anniversary of the Resignation of Sheikh Hasina, on 5 August 2025, the revolution received constitutional acknowledgment with the announcement of the July Declaration, and it has been described as the world's first Gen Z revolution.

July Uprising Timeline

10 July

Shahbagh Blockade Begins

Students marched from Dhaka University, blocked Shahbagh, halting transport across the capital.

14 July

Midnight Protest After Hasina’s Remark

Students launched a midnight protest following Sheikh Hasina’s controversial “Razakar” comment.

16 July

First Martyr: Abu Sayed

Police firing in Rangpur killed student Abu Sayed, marking a turning point in the movement.

18 July

Nationwide Shutdown

Internet blackout, metro suspension, and mass clashes erupted across Bangladesh.

5 August

Sheikh Hasina Resigns

Following mass protests, Sheikh Hasina resigned and fled the country.
